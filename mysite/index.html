Welcome to my personal web-site!


I am an Ecuadorian biologist with expertise in molecular biology, biochemistry, microbiology, biodiversity conservation, and scientific research. In 2004, I was awarded a BSc. in Biological Sciences from Pontificia Universidad Catolica del Ecuador (Quito-Ecuador). Later, I studied a Master in Management and Conservation of Tropical Biodiversity from Universidad San Pablo (Sevilla-Spain) in 2007, and Master in Microbiology from Universidad San Francisco de Quito (Quito-Ecuador) in 2012. However, I realize that in order to reach my objectives as academic researcher I needed a PhD. Thus, I came to Queen’s University Belfast to study a PhD in Pharmacy, which I just finished!
Why I decided to come to QUB? Easy, the Natural Drug discovery group, led by Professor Chris Shaw, is one of the best in its field; Queen’s is a well renewed higher research institution; and Belfast a great place to live!
Why I am particularly interested in natural drug discovery? Well, I became interested in the studying the chemical hidden in the frog when my professor of Zoology introduce me epibatidine -an alkaloid identified in the skin of the Ecuadorian poison frog Epipedobates tricolor Epibatidine acts as a pain killer, but it acts under nicotinic receptors instead of opiod receptos. Therefore not being addictive!. An analog of epibatidine named ABT-494 is in development phase II for the treatment of patients with reumatoidea arthritis. Since the day I hear this story, I decided to study this molecules!
