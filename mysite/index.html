Welcome to my personal web-site!


I am an Ecuadorian biologist interested in Microbiology and Biochemistry. I studied a Bachelor in Biological Sciences in the "Pontificia Universidad Católica del Ecuador" (1998-2004) in Quito-Ecuador. Later in 2007, I when to Spain to study a short Master of Management and Conservation Biology in the Tropics in the "Universidad San Pablo CEU" in Seville. After that I came back to Ecuador and decide to keep studing a bit more. This time I focussed on Microbiology. My Master in Microbiology was issued by the "Universidad San Francisco de Quito" (2008-2010). However, I realized that to be teaching in the University and doing research at the same time I really need a PhD. So, here I am. Finishig my PhD in Pharmacy in Queen's University Belfast! (2013-2016).
I have been studying the skin secretion of frogs since my undergrad days. I became interested in the studying the chemical hidden in the frog when my professor of Zoology introduce me epibatidine -an alkaloid identified in the skin of the Ecuadorian poison frog Epipedobates tricolor Epibatidine acts as a pain killer, but it acts under nicotinic receptors instead of opiod receptos. Therefore not being addictive!. An analog of epiatidine named ABT-494 is in development phase II for the treatment of patients with reumatoidea arthritis.
